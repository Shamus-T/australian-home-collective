# Laundry Product Category Plan

## 1. Purpose

This document prepares future Laundry product-category sections for Australian Home Collective.

No product links are being added yet. No affiliate links are being added yet. No prices are being added yet. No product testing claims should be made unless genuine testing has actually happened and can be explained clearly.

The site's current strength is practical before-you-buy guidance. Future Laundry content should help readers measure, check, sort and plan before buying storage, drying, basket, cleaning-product or appliance-adjacent products.

Future product sections should support the guide content rather than replace it. Australian homes, rentals, apartments and small laundries should be assumed unless a page clearly says otherwise.

Use `docs/content-plans/product-section-templates.md` as the reusable structure before adding future product-category blocks. Public product blocks should not be added until the Laundry cluster review is complete and the commercial setup, disclosure placement and update process are ready.

Higher-risk categories such as heated airers, chemical storage, appliance storage, dryer placement and washing-machine clearance require cautious wording and a clear update process before any public product links or comparison sections are added.

## 2. Commercial principles

- Product sections should come after useful buying guidance, not before it.
- Do not claim "best" unless the claim is justified and maintained.
- Do not claim products were tested unless they were genuinely tested.
- Do not use current prices unless there is a reliable update process.
- Do not create fake urgency.
- Do not recommend one product for every household.
- Prefer product types, fit checks and use cases first.
- Add affiliate disclosure only when actual affiliate links are present.
- Product sections should still help readers who do not click shopping links.
- Australian context and Australian English should be used by default.
- Be cautious with safety-related claims around dryers, electrical items, stacked appliances, children, pets, chemicals and ventilation.

## 4. Future product-section format

### Product category section template

#### What this product type may help with

Explain the household problem the product type can solve. Keep it practical and tied to laundry routines.

#### What to measure first

List practical measurements needed before buying. Examples may include floor space, door clearance, shelf width, appliance clearance, tub cabinet depth, basket height, rack footprint and folded storage size.

#### What to check before buying

Include fit, cleaning, durability, access, rental suitability, safety considerations and maintenance checks.

#### Best suited to

Describe household types or use cases. Examples may include small laundries, renters, apartment laundries, family laundry loads, indoor drying needs or homes with limited outdoor drying space.

#### Avoid if

Explain when the product type may be unnecessary or unsuitable. Include cases where sorting, measuring, clearing duplicates or changing routines should happen first.

#### Renter notes

Mention removable and non-permanent considerations where relevant. Be careful with adhesive, drilling, over-door racks, wall-mounted products and anything that may damage surfaces.

#### Cleaning and maintenance notes

Explain likely cleaning or moisture issues. Include lint, detergent spills, dampness, rust, mould risk, dust, blocked access and cleaning around appliances.

#### Safety and placement notes

Add cautious, practical notes where relevant, especially for drying, chemicals, appliances, stacked items and ventilation.

#### Related Australian Home Collective guides

List relevant internal guide links once guides exist.

#### Future product link area

Product links may be added later if suitable affiliate/commercial links are available and properly disclosed.
