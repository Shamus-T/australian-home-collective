# Bathroom Product Category Plan

## Purpose

This document maps future Bathroom product categories for Australian Home Collective guides and later product sections.

No product links are being added yet. No affiliate links are being added yet. No prices are being added yet. No product testing claims should be made unless genuine testing has actually happened and can be described clearly.

The Bathroom cluster should support the site's before-you-buy strategy. Future Bathroom content should help readers measure, sort, check access, understand surface and moisture limits, and avoid poor purchases before buying organisers, shelves, caddies, hooks, hampers, cabinets or storage containers.

Bathroom product sections should support practical guidance rather than replace it. Readers should still get value from a guide even if no commercial links are present or clicked.

## Bathroom content strategy

Bathroom content should focus on practical checks for Australian homes, apartments, townhouses, rentals, shared bathrooms, family bathrooms and small ensuites.

The Bathroom cluster's role is to help readers:

- measure small bathroom spaces before buying storage
- check moisture, ventilation, tile or wall surfaces, rental limits and storage access
- sort toiletries, towels, cleaning products, medicines, cosmetics and daily-use items before buying more storage
- avoid overbuying baskets, caddies, shelves, cabinets or adhesive organisers
- consider wet areas, glass, mirrors, slippery floors, children, pets and shared household routines
- keep storage choices tied to real behaviour rather than filling every wall, drawer or bench

Bathroom articles should stay practical and cautious. The aim is not to design, install, certify or waterproof a bathroom storage system. The aim is to help readers notice fit, access, suitability, moisture and risk before buying.

## Bathroom safety and wording rules

Bathroom storage has higher wording risk than ordinary household storage because it can involve moisture, mould, ventilation, glass, tiles, adhesives, drilling, waterproofing, medicines, razors, cleaning products, cosmetics, children, pets, renters, small spaces and wet-area surfaces.

Internal wording rules:

- Avoid plumbing advice.
- Avoid electrical advice.
- Avoid waterproofing advice.
- Avoid structural advice.
- Avoid medical advice.
- Avoid chemical-storage advice.
- Avoid mould-remediation advice.
- Do not tell readers that adhesive hooks, suction storage, drilled fixtures, wall cabinets, shower caddies or shelves are safe or suitable for every bathroom.
- Refer readers to manufacturer guidance, load ratings, surface suitability, installation instructions, lease or body corporate approval where relevant, and qualified trades where needed.
- Be cautious with medicines, razors, blades, cleaning products, mould, damp towels, glass, mirrors, slippery floors, children and pets.
- Do not imply all bathrooms are suitable for drilling, adhesive products, suction products, over-toilet units or wall-mounted cabinets.
- Use cautious wording such as "may suit", "may help", "check first", "if suitable for the surface" and "follow manufacturer guidance".

## Product category risk levels

### Low-risk categories

Low-risk Bathroom categories usually involve simple, non-permanent storage that is easy to move, does not rely on wall fixing, and is not used for medicines, chemicals, sharp items or electrical products.

Examples:

- vanity drawer organisers
- makeup trays
- simple toiletry baskets
- guest bathroom baskets
- non-fixed towel baskets
- small containers for daily-use items

Handle these with normal before-you-buy checks. Avoid implying more containers will fix clutter on their own, and remind readers to sort products before buying more storage.

### Medium-risk categories

Medium-risk Bathroom categories may involve moisture, glass, tile surfaces, adhesive or suction attachment, shared household use, cleaning products, access, stability or rental surfaces.

Examples:

- under-sink bathroom organisers
- bathroom cabinet organisers
- freestanding shelves
- shower caddies
- adhesive hooks
- suction storage
- towel rails and hooks
- laundry hamper storage for bathrooms
- renter-friendly bathroom storage

Use cautious wording around moisture, ventilation, surface suitability, load ratings, cleaning, access, rental approval and product instructions.

### High-risk / caution categories

High-risk Bathroom categories involve wet areas, drilling, waterproofing, wall mounting, glass, medicines, razors, cleaning chemicals, hair tools, electrical products, mould-prone storage or child/pet access.

Examples:

- wall-mounted bathroom cabinets
- drilled towel rails and hooks
- over-toilet storage in tight bathrooms
- medicine and first-aid storage
- cleaning chemical storage
- hair tool storage
- glass shelves and mirrors
- electrical storage near wet areas
- anything that may affect waterproofing, tiles, plumbing, electrical outlets or rental approval

These categories need cautious, limited language. Do not give installation, medical, chemical-safety, electrical, waterproofing or mould-remediation advice. Public content should focus on questions to check, measurements to take, and reminders to follow manufacturer guidance, product labels, rental requirements or qualified advice where needed.

## Future Bathroom product categories

### 1. Under-sink bathroom organisers

What the category covers:

Shelves, baskets, trays, pull-out organisers and pipe-friendly units used inside bathroom vanity cabinets.

Good fit for:

Bathrooms with enclosed vanity storage, toiletries, spare products, toilet paper, hair care, cleaning cloths or low-risk daily-use items that need grouping.

What to measure/check before buying:

- cabinet width, depth and height
- pipe position and trap clearance
- door swing and hinge placement
- leaks, dampness or condensation
- shelf height and drawer clearance
- whether cleaning products or medicines are stored there
- how easily items can be removed for cleaning

Common mistakes:

- buying organisers before checking pipe clearance
- blocking access to plumbing
- using deep containers that hide small items
- storing too many leak-prone products together
- assuming every under-sink cabinet is dry

Risk level:

Medium.

Safe wording notes for future public content:

Avoid plumbing advice and universal-fit claims. Refer readers to pipe clearance, moisture checks and manufacturer guidance. Be careful around cleaning products, medicines and child/pet access.

### 2. Bathroom vanity drawer organisers

Drawer inserts, dividers, trays and small containers for daily toiletries, makeup, grooming items, razors, hair accessories and spare bathroom items.

What to measure/check before buying:

- drawer width, depth and height
- item sizes

- wipeability
- whether organisers slide around
- which items are used daily versus occasionally

Common mistakes:

- buying a tray set before measuring drawer height
- keeping expired or unused products
- mixing sharp items with daily toiletries
- creating too many tiny compartments that are annoying to maintain

Risk level:

Low to Medium.

Safe wording notes for future public content:

Keep advice sorting-led. Use cautious wording for razors, blades and child access. Avoid medical or hygiene claims.

- side clearance

- floor level
- stability

Common mistakes:

- ignoring stability
- storing heavy items high
- making cleaning harder
- assuming every toilet area suits overhead storage

Risk level:

High.

Safe wording notes for future public content:

Use cautious language around stability, clearance and fixing. Refer to manufacturer instructions and avoid recommending over-toilet storage for very tight bathrooms.

## Categories to treat carefully or avoid

These categories need extra caution and should not be casually recommended:

- wall-mounted bathroom cabinets
- drilled towel rails and hooks
- adhesive shelves in wet areas
- suction products in showers
- over-toilet storage in very tight bathrooms
- medicine storage
- cleaning chemical storage
- hair tool and electrical storage
- mould-prone storage
- glass shelves and mirrors
- anything that may affect waterproofing, tiles, plumbing, electrical outlets or rental approval

Future public content should not make plumbing, electrical, waterproofing, structural, medical, chemical-storage, mould-remediation or safety-certification claims. If a category depends on surface, fixing, wet-area, chemical, medicine, electrical or rental suitability, the guide should keep advice to measurement and suitability questions, then point readers to manufacturer guidance, product labels, approval requirements or qualified trades where needed.
