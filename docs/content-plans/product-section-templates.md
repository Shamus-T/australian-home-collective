# Product Section Templates

Purpose: create reusable future product-section templates for Australian Home Collective guide articles.

Before drafting article content or future product sections, use `docs/content-plans/editorial-blueprint.md` as the reader-first editorial standard. The article should help readers decide whether to buy, delay, reuse, repair, choose a lower-cost option, or avoid buying altogether where appropriate.

Product sections may be added later only when the commercial setup is ready. No affiliate links are being added now, no product recommendations are being published now, and no product prices should be used unless there is a reliable update process.

Do not make "tested by us" claims unless genuine testing has happened and can be explained clearly. Product sections should support the guide content, not replace practical advice. Readers should still get value from the article even if they do not click product links.

## Global product-section rules

- Product sections should come after practical buying guidance.
- Do not lead an article with product links.
- Do not claim "best" unless the claim is justified, maintained and clearly framed.
- Do not use fake urgency.
- Do not make one product sound suitable for every home.
- Do not claim personal testing unless testing actually happened.
- Do not use current prices unless they can be reviewed and updated.
- Avoid strong safety claims.
- Use cautious wording for chemicals, electrical items, appliance placement, child/pet access, moisture, mould, ventilation and installation.
- Add affiliate disclosure only when actual affiliate links are present.
- If affiliate links are added later, the disclosure should be visible before or near commercial links according to the site's disclosure approach.
- Use Australian English.
- Use product categories and use cases before specific product recommendations.
- Recommend products based on suitability rather than commission potential.
- Present advantages and disadvantages honestly.
- Recommend against unnecessary purchases where appropriate.
- External links should be used only where they add genuine editorial value and must follow the editorial blueprint's ABC rule and new-tab behaviour.

## Product-template risk levels

### Low risk

Examples:

- baskets
- simple bins
- shelf inserts
- non-permanent drawer organisers
- simple pantry or laundry organisers

Avoid language that says one product is right for every home, guarantees clutter will be solved, or implies a product is necessary before the reader has sorted and measured the space.

### Medium risk

Examples:

- under-sink organisers
- over-door racks
- adhesive storage
- drying racks
- food containers
- cleaning-product storage
- renter-specific products

Avoid promising that adhesive products will not damage surfaces, that storage is safe for all rental situations, that damp areas will stay dry, or that a product will suit every cabinet, door, pipe layout or household routine.

### High risk

Examples:

- electrical products
- heated airers
- washing machines
- dryers
- appliance stacking
- chemical storage claims
- child/pet safety claims
- Australian-made claims requiring verification

Avoid electrical, plumbing, appliance installation, tenancy, chemical-safety or child/pet safety advice. Avoid current-price comparisons, unsupported Australian-made claims and any statement that sounds like a safety guarantee. Use cautious wording and refer readers to product labels, manufacturer instructions, rental agreements, appliance guidance or verified source material where relevant.
