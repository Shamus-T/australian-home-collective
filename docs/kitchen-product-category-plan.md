# Kitchen Product Category Plan

## 1. Purpose

This document prepares future Kitchen product-category sections for Australian Home Collective.

No product links are being added yet. No affiliate links are being added yet. No prices are being added yet. No product testing claims should be made unless genuine testing has actually happened and can be described clearly.

The current strength of the Kitchen cluster is practical before-you-buy guidance. The guides help readers measure, sort, check and decide before buying organisers, containers, storage products or appliances.

Future product sections should support the guide content rather than replace it. They should help readers understand product types, use cases, measurements and suitability before any commercial link is introduced.

## 2. Commercial principles

- Product sections should come after useful buying guidance, not before it.
- Do not claim "best" unless the claim is justified and maintained.
- Do not claim products were tested unless they were genuinely tested.
- Do not use current prices unless there is a reliable update process.
- Do not create fake urgency.
- Do not recommend one product for every household.
- Prefer product types, fit checks and use cases first.
- Add affiliate disclosure only when actual affiliate links are present.
- Product sections should still help readers who do not click shopping links.
- Australian context should be assumed unless a page clearly says otherwise.

## 3. Future product-section format

### Product category section template

#### What this product type may help with

Explain the household problem the product type can solve. Keep the explanation practical and tied to everyday kitchen use.

#### What to measure first

List the practical measurements needed before buying. Use relevant dimensions such as shelf depth, drawer height, cupboard width, door clearance, bench depth, pipe clearance or appliance height.

#### What to check before buying

Include fit, cleaning, durability, access, rental suitability and maintenance checks. Do not assume a product will suit every household.

#### Best suited to

Describe household types or use cases. Examples may include renters, small kitchens, families, apartment kitchens, batch-cooking households or households with daily lunchbox routines.

#### Avoid if

Explain when the product type may be unnecessary or unsuitable. This should include cases where sorting, clearing, measuring or reducing duplicates should happen first.

#### Renter notes

Mention removable and non-permanent considerations where relevant. Be careful with adhesive, drilling, door-mounted storage and anything that may damage cabinetry or paint.

#### Cleaning and maintenance notes

Explain likely cleaning issues. Include crumbs, oils, moisture, food contact, dust, spills, bin odours or hard-to-reach areas where relevant.

#### Related Australian Home Collective guides

List relevant internal guide links so readers can move from product type to broader planning.

#### Future product link area

Product links may be added later if suitable affiliate/commercial links are available and properly disclosed.

## 6. Priority order for future monetisation

1. Pantry organisers
2. Drawer organisers
3. Food containers and lid storage
4. Renter-friendly kitchen storage
5. Under-sink storage
6. Bench-space storage
7. Small appliance storage
8. Small appliance recommendations

This order starts with lower-risk storage categories that match the current before-you-buy guide format. Pantry organisers, drawer organisers and container storage can be presented through measurements, use cases and fit checks without making strong performance claims.

Appliance-heavy recommendations should wait until the site has stronger trust signals and a reliable update process. Appliance specifications, availability and models can change quickly, and specific recommendations can date faster than storage guidance.

Storage products fit the current article format better than specific appliance reviews because they can be discussed through practical questions: Will it fit? Is it easy to clean? Does it suit a rental? Does it solve the real storage problem?

## 7. What not to monetise yet

- Specific appliance "best" lists
- Current-price comparisons
- Safety-sensitive child/pet storage claims
- Strong "Australian made" claims without verification
- Product testing claims
- Anything that requires frequent updates before we have a process
- Products with complex installation, electrical or plumbing implications
- Product categories where material-safety claims would need careful verification
