## How we develop recommendations

Australian Home Collective helps Australians make informed home-planning and buying decisions.

Our recommendations are based on practical suitability rather than product popularity, marketing claims, sales rankings or commission rates.

Instead of asking only `Which product is best?`, we ask:

- Will it suit the reader's home and intended use?
- Will it fit the available space and delivery route?
- What is required to install and use it properly?
- What trade-offs should the buyer understand?
- What may matter during ownership and after-sales support?
- What commonly overlooked problems can be avoided?

Our goal is to give readers a useful framework for their own circumstances.

## Planning comes first

Every guide begins with the decision and the setting in which a product will be used.

Before discussing brands or models, we encourage readers to understand the available space, access, installation requirements, compatibility, maintenance, future needs and practical constraints.

A well-planned purchase is more valuable than an impulse purchase.

## What we may consider

The factors below are used when relevant. Not every factor applies to every category.

### Practical suitability

Does the option suit the intended room, household, layout and purpose?

### Dimensions and fit

Will the product fit the installation space? Have operating clearances, delivery access, packaged dimensions and full door or drawer movement been considered?

### Compatibility

Will it work with existing furniture, appliances, services, fittings, systems and room layouts?

### Installation requirements

Does the product require ventilation, plumbing, electrical work, structural fixing, specialised access or professional installation?

### Usability

Can the product be opened, adjusted, loaded, cleaned and used as intended in the proposed position?

### Long-term ownership

What should readers consider about running costs, maintenance access, durability indicators, consumables, repairability, replacement costs and future household needs?

### After-sales support

What should readers investigate about written warranty terms, local service coverage, support channels, spare-parts availability and the party responsible for support?

We teach readers what to check. We do not make broad, unsupported claims about the quality of a brand's service.

### Safety and authoritative requirements

Where relevant, we highlight manufacturer instructions, recognised Australian guidance and situations in which a qualified professional should be consulted.

## Evidence and sources

We may use:

- current manufacturer specifications and manuals;
- Australian government and regulator information;
- recognised safety or efficiency guidance;
- clearly described first-hand observations where available; and
- practical planning principles.

We prefer primary sources and qualify information that varies or cannot be fully verified.

## What we do not do

Australian Home Collective is not a product-testing laboratory unless a specific article clearly describes the testing conducted.

We do not:

- claim to have tested, owned or inspected products when we have not;
- rank products solely by commission, popularity or advertising relationships;
- declare a product suitable without considering context;
- treat marketing claims as independent evidence; or
- reduce a complex decision to an unexplained numerical score.

## How recommendations are expressed

Different homes, budgets and priorities often produce different reasonable choices. We therefore explain which circumstances an option may suit and what trade-offs come with it.

Where products are named, selection should be connected to transparent criteria. A named product is not automatically suitable for every reader.

## Ratings and scores

Australian Home Collective does not assign arbitrary scores or `Editor's Choice` labels.

If a structured score or comparison system is introduced in future, its criteria, weighting, evidence and limitations must be published clearly.

## Commercial relationships

Affiliate or commercial relationships do not determine our methodology or conclusions. Material relationships are disclosed, and useful guidance must remain useful even if a reader does not purchase through a link.

## Interactive planning tools

Our tools help readers identify potential considerations and measurement conflicts. They do not provide engineering, installation, safety or delivery approval.

Each tool should explain:

- what it assesses;
- which information it needs;
- what assumptions it makes;
- what it cannot determine; and
- when further verification is recommended.

## Consumer rights information

Where ownership and warranty considerations are relevant, we may direct readers to authoritative information about Australian Consumer Law. We do not provide legal advice or decide how the law applies to an individual situation.

See the ACCC's [consumer rights and guarantees](https://www.accc.gov.au/consumers/buying-products-and-services/consumer-rights-and-guarantees) guidance.

## Continuous improvement

We review and update content when products, guidance, standards, reader needs or our own practical understanding change. Substantive improvements may update the editorial review date.

## Guiding principle

> **Will this help someone make a better decision for their home?**

If the answer is no, it does not belong in Australian Home Collective.
