## Our approach

Australian Home Collective exists to help Australians make informed home-planning and buying decisions before they spend money.

Our guides focus on practical considerations such as measurements, planning, compatibility, delivery, installation, ownership and long-term suitability.

Rather than telling readers what to buy, we explain what to consider so they can choose products that suit their homes, circumstances and budgets.

## Editorial independence

Editorial decisions are made independently.

Commercial relationships, affiliate partnerships and advertising opportunities do not determine the topics we cover or the conclusions we reach.

If a product, category or approach may not suit a particular situation, we will say so—even when that means suggesting readers delay a purchase or consider another approach.

Maintaining reader trust is more important than maximising short-term revenue.

## How we develop guides

Our articles draw on practical planning principles, published specifications, authoritative guidance where appropriate and common buying considerations relevant to Australian households.

Many guides focus on details that are easy to overlook, including:

- measurements and clearances;
- delivery access;
- installation requirements;
- room layout and compatibility;
- maintenance and service access;
- after-sales support considerations; and
- long-term usability.

Where requirements vary between products, manufacturers, installers or homes, we explain that variation rather than presenting one answer as universal.

## Accuracy and editorial responsibility

We make every reasonable effort to publish accurate, clear and current information. Every article is reviewed before publication, and Australian Home Collective accepts responsibility for the content it publishes.

Products, specifications, standards and manufacturer requirements can change. Readers should confirm critical measurements, current specifications and installation requirements before buying or installing a product.

If we become aware of an error or outdated information, we review and correct it in line with our Corrections & Content Review Policy.

## Sources

We prefer primary, current and authoritative sources for factual, legal, safety and product-specific information. These may include Australian government agencies, recognised authorities and manufacturer documentation.

Where an official Australian source is better placed to explain a topic, we provide useful context and direct readers to that source rather than presenting ourselves as a substitute.

## Commercial relationships

Australian Home Collective may participate in affiliate or referral programs. If a reader chooses to purchase a product or service through a qualifying link, Australian Home Collective may receive a commission.

These commissions help fund the publication. They do not determine our editorial standards or conclusions, and we do not recommend a product solely because it may generate revenue.

Material commercial relationships will be disclosed clearly.

## Sponsored content

If sponsored content is accepted, it will be clearly identified. Sponsorship does not remove the requirement to meet our editorial standards, and sponsored material will not be presented as independent editorial without appropriate disclosure.

## Product guidance and recommendations

Australian Home Collective is not a product-testing laboratory unless a specific article clearly states the testing performed.

Our usual role is to help readers understand:

- what to measure;
- what to compare;
- what questions to ask;
- which features matter in different circumstances;
- what ownership and support considerations may apply; and
- which common mistakes can be avoided before purchase.

Different homes, budgets and priorities can lead to different reasonable choices.

## Interactive planning tools

Our planning tools assist with planning and decision-making. They do not replace current manufacturer information, site-specific assessment, delivery-provider requirements, qualified tradespeople or professional advice where required.

Results depend on the information entered and the assumptions explained within each tool. Users should independently verify important measurements and requirements before purchasing, delivering or installing a product.

## Consumer information

Where relevant, we may explain in general terms that automatic consumer guarantees under Australian Consumer Law are separate from voluntary warranties. We do not provide legal advice or interpret individual disputes.

For authoritative information, readers should refer to the ACCC's [consumer rights and guarantees](https://www.accc.gov.au/consumers/buying-products-and-services/consumer-rights-and-guarantees) guidance.
