## Purpose

This standard sets the minimum limitation wording for calculators, planners, visualisers, decision aids and interactive checklists. It works with the [Planning Tool Standard](../tools/planning-tool-standard.md).

## Required meaning

Every tool must make clear, in wording suited to that tool, that:

- it assists planning and does not guarantee delivery, installation, fit, safety or compliance;
- results depend on the information entered and the assumptions stated;
- it does not replace current manufacturer instructions, provider requirements, a site-specific assessment, a qualified trade or other professional advice where required;
- unknown information remains an unresolved check rather than being guessed; and
- important measurements and requirements must be independently confirmed before a purchase, delivery or installation.

## Placement

Limitations must appear:

1. before the reader enters information;
2. beside any input or assumption that could be misunderstood;
3. with the result; and
4. in any printed, saved or shared summary.

A footer disclaimer alone is not sufficient.

## Result language

Use bounded wording such as:

- `No conflict found in the checks completed`;
- `More information is needed`;
- `A potential measurement conflict was identified`; or
- `Professional or provider confirmation is recommended`.

Do not use `approved`, `certified`, `guaranteed`, `safe`, `pass`, `will fit` or similar wording unless the statement is supported by a competent authority and is genuinely within the tool's scope.

## Tool-specific review

Before launch, the functional specification must record:

- the exact decision the tool assists with;
- what is and is not assessed;
- every material assumption and source;
- the approved limitation and result wording;
- the situations that require professional or provider confirmation; and
- the disable, correction and withdrawal path.

Higher-risk wording must receive appropriate editorial and legal review. The review must assess the real tool rather than approve a generic disclaimer in isolation.

## Maintenance

Re-review the wording after a material change to calculations, inputs, output, sources, storage, analytics, professional referrals or intended use. Dates are not advanced for cosmetic changes.
