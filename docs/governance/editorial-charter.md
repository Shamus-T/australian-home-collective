## Our purpose

Australian Home Collective exists to help Australians make confident home-planning and buying decisions before they spend money.

We do not tell people what to buy. We help them understand what to measure, compare, plan and consider so they can choose products that suit their homes, needs and budgets.

Our success is measured by the expensive mistakes readers avoid, not simply by the products they purchase.

## Our vision

To become Australia's most trusted independent home-planning resource.

When Australians are planning a purchase for their home, Australian Home Collective should be one of the first resources they think to use.

## Our motto

> **Plan Better. Buy Smarter.**

## Editorial pillars

### Independence

Editorial decisions are not dictated by advertisers, affiliate partners or sales incentives.

### Accuracy

We do not manufacture certainty. If an answer depends on the product, manufacturer, home or circumstances, we explain that dependence. When uncertainty remains, we say so.

### Practicality

Every guide, tool and resource should solve a real planning problem. Advice should extend beyond information available in a product listing or specification sheet.

### Transparency

Readers should be able to understand how guidance is developed, when commercial relationships exist, what assumptions have been made and which limitations apply.

### Respect

We write for capable adults in clear Australian English. We explain technical matters without talking down to readers or pretending complex decisions are simple.

### Longevity

We prefer useful, maintainable resources over fleeting trends. Content should remain valuable for years or have a clear review plan where information changes.

## What Australian Home Collective is

Australian Home Collective is:

- an independent Australian home-planning publication;
- a buying-guide and measurement resource;
- a source of practical planning tools and checklists; and
- a consumer education resource.

Australian Home Collective is not:

- a coupon or deals site;
- a product catalogue;
- a laboratory claiming tests it has not performed;
- a rankings factory; or
- a general lifestyle magazine.

## What we will never do

We will never:

- invent product testing, ownership or personal experience;
- publish fake reviews or fabricated customer stories;
- rank or recommend products for commission alone;
- exaggerate benefits or manufacture urgency;
- knowingly publish misleading headlines;
- conceal material commercial relationships;
- recommend an option we know is unsuitable for the stated circumstances; or
- trade long-term reader trust for short-term revenue.

## The no-regret purchase principle

Every guide should reduce the risk of buyer's remorse by helping readers think through fit, access, compatibility, installation, ownership and support before purchasing.

Buying is only successful when the product can be delivered, installed, used and maintained as intended—not merely when it has been paid for.

## Practical and retail insight

Where relevant, content should address issues commonly overlooked during real purchasing decisions, including:

- delivery access and packaged dimensions;
- installation constraints and service locations;
- sizing, clearance and compatibility;
- maintenance and repair access;
- after-sales support and spare-parts availability; and
- long-term usability and changing household needs.

These insights must be expressed as practical considerations, not invented anecdotes or unsupported claims of expertise.

## Editorial responsibility

Australian Home Collective is responsible for the accuracy, clarity and quality of everything it publishes.

Every item must be reviewed before publication. Information that cannot be verified should be qualified or omitted. Errors should be corrected when identified.

## Tools and planning resources

New features should make the publication more useful, not simply larger. Priority should be given to:

- planning tools and calculators;
- visualisers;
- measurement planners;
- printable checklists; and
- transparent comparison resources.

Tools assist decisions. They do not replace professional judgement, manufacturer requirements or site-specific assessment, and they must not promise guaranteed outcomes.

## The AHC Test

Before anything is published, ask:

1. Does it solve a real problem?
2. Could it help someone avoid an expensive mistake?
3. Would we confidently recommend it to a customer or family member?
4. Is it likely to remain useful in five years?
5. Does it build trust?

If any answer is no, revise the work or do not publish it.

## The customer question

> If someone walked into a homemaker centre tomorrow, would this help them make a better decision?

## The golden rule

> **If a decision improves short-term revenue but reduces long-term trust, we do not do it.**

## Definition of success

A successful resource should leave a reader thinking:

> **I'm glad I checked this before buying.**
