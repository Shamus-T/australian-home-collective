## Authority and scope

This manual defines the standards, policies and workflows governing publications, articles, planning tools and digital resources produced by Australian Home Collective.

It is the index for the publishing foundation. Detailed requirements live in the linked documents and future standards identified below.

Australian Home Collective is a registered Australian business and an independent Australian home-planning publication. Its mission is to help Australians plan better and buy smarter. Reader trust takes priority over short-term revenue.

## 3. Publication standards

### Article standard

Every guide must:

- serve a clear reader need;
- explain relevant measurements, comparisons and trade-offs;
- identify commonly overlooked considerations where appropriate;
- distinguish facts, guidance and assumptions;
- contain practical value beyond product specifications;
- avoid manufactured expertise;
- include useful internal links and accurate metadata;
- use high-trust external sources only and open external links in a new tab;
- use accessible headings, images, tables and controls; and
- pass the AHC Test before publication.

`Our Take`, checklists and related guides should be used when they genuinely improve the article rather than as compulsory filler.

### Planning-tool standard

Every planning tool must:

- solve a genuine planning problem;
- define what it assesses and what it cannot assess;
- make assumptions and required inputs clear;
- validate inputs and handle missing or implausible values safely;
- avoid approvals, guarantees and false precision;
- use cautious, explanatory result language;
- provide visible, tool-specific limitations;
- work on common mobile and desktop devices;
- meet accessibility requirements;
- be tested before release and after material changes; and
- provide a printable or saveable summary where useful.

**Detailed Planning Tool Standard:** [Active, version 1.1](../tools/planning-tool-standard.md).

### Buying-framework standard

Buying frameworks should follow a logical planning sequence, explain decision points, acknowledge trade-offs and connect readers to relevant guides and tools.

**Detailed standard:** Pending.

### Downloadable-resource standard

Printable resources must add genuine value, use accessible language, work on A4 paper, include a version and review date, and avoid unnecessary branding or visual clutter.

**Detailed standard:** Pending.

## 4. Legal and risk

The [Tool Disclaimer Standard](../legal/tool-disclaimer-standard.md) sets the minimum editorial wording for planning tools. Terms and privacy review notes are maintained in [`docs/legal/`](../legal/README.md).

Internal standards are not a substitute for advice from an appropriately qualified Australian solicitor. Legal guidance should be added only when a real publishing or tool risk requires it, not as speculative bureaucracy.

## 5. Publishing workflow

Every substantial editorial item follows this lifecycle:

1. Idea and reader need
2. Research and source assessment
3. Outline
4. Draft
5. Publication edit
6. Fact verification
7. Practical-value and knowledge-base review
8. Internal-link and metadata review
9. Accessibility review
10. Legal or risk review where required
11. Publish
12. Monitor, correct and editorially review

Stages may be combined for minor updates, but no published item may skip the checks relevant to its risk and purpose.

## 6. Interactive tools

Interactive tools should follow a consistent experience where appropriate:

1. Explain the planning problem.
2. Gather measurements or choices.
3. Explain why each important input matters.
4. Identify possible conflicts and unknowns.
5. Present an assessment without guaranteeing an outcome.
6. Provide next checks and a useful summary.

The [Retail Knowledge Base](../knowledge/retail-knowledge-base.md) should inform inputs, explanations, warnings and commonly overlooked considerations.

## 7. Partnerships

### Affiliate relationships

Affiliate links support the publication; they do not determine editorial conclusions. Partners and products should be relevant to readers and suitable for the context in which they appear. Material relationships must be disclosed clearly.

Until the commercial workflow is formally approved, public content must not include named model recommendations, retailer links or affiliate links. Future links must be contextual, verified and recorded through the approved commercial catalogue and review process.

### Sponsored content

If sponsored content is accepted, it must be clearly identified and still meet the publication's editorial standards. Sponsorship does not permit unsupported claims or hidden control over editorial conclusions.

## 8. Business development

The default priority is:

1. Maintain and improve existing content.
2. Strengthen reader trust and public standards.
3. Build useful planning tools and resources.
4. Introduce responsible commercial partnerships.
5. Expand into new categories or publications only when quality can be sustained.

## 9. AHC Planning Centre

The Planning Centre is intended to bring together guides, interactive tools, visualisers, calculators, planning sheets and buying frameworks.

Every proposed feature must answer:

> **Does this help Australians make a better home-planning or buying decision?**

If not, it does not belong in the Planning Centre.

## 10. Quality assurance and review

- Display review dates only after genuine editorial review.
- Do not advance dates automatically.
- Correct significant factual errors promptly and transparently.
- Retest tools after logic, dependency or browser-support changes.
- Review higher-risk and higher-traffic resources more frequently.
- Record substantive changes in Git and, where useful to readers, on the published page.
